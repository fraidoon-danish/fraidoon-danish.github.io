Started on a kitchen table. Still fixing things the same way.

City Computer Store began as one technician repairing laptops for neighbours in Bridgeland. Thirteen years later, it's a three-person bench that still answers its own phone.

Our story

Built for people who'd rather ask a person than a chatbot.

Marcus Whitfield started fixing computers out of a garage in 2013 after getting laid off from an IT support contract. The idea was simple: charge a fair price, explain the problem in plain language, and never replace a part that didn't need replacing.

That approach is still how the shop runs. We're not a franchise, we don't work off commission, and every quote is written by the technician who'll actually do the work. If a $40 fix solves it, that's what goes on the invoice — not a $400 upsell.

Today the shop handles everything from single-screw repairs to fully custom workstations for local studios, but the bench is still walk-in friendly, and the coffee's still on.
